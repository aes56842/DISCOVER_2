17Jun2026

Reciprocal Quantum Logic (RQL): Controlled Dual Rail Qubit Architecture

Dr. Anthony Przybysz Senior Staff Physicist, Northrop Grumman Corporation 7:12 PM PT · Zoom
This talk presents recent advances in RQL-based qubit control circuits, with a focus on dual-rail qubit architecture and how controlled entanglement operations can be implemented in a cryogenic digital environment. Slides available for download.

08Nov2025

SuperSoCC: Designing a Three-Stage Cryogenic System-on-Chip

Dr. Murali Annavaram Professor, USC Viterbi School of Engineering 12:00 PM PT · Zoom
Overview of the SuperSoCC architecture — integrating SFQ compute cores, cryogenic SRAM, and DRAM at different temperature tiers. This talk covers the memory subsystem design and the challenges of room-temperature I/O interfacing.
▶ Watch Slides ↓
22May2025

AQFP Logic at Scale: Efficiency Limits and Fabrication Progress

Prof. Nobuyuki Yoshikawa Yokohama National University 6:00 PM PT · Zoom
This talk reviews the current state of AQFP (Adiabatic Quantum Flux Parametron) logic, discussing the energy efficiency limits achievable with current Nb fabrication processes and progress toward higher junction-count circuits for general-purpose compute.

14Oct2024

Niobium Junction Fabrication: From Process to Circuit Performance

Prof. Grace Xing Cornell University, School of ECE 12:00 PM PT · Zoom
Covers the niobium trilayer fabrication process used across DISCoVER partner institutions, with a focus on junction uniformity across wafers and its implications for large-scale SFQ circuit yield. Includes live demo of test-chip measurement data.

03Apr2024

Distributed ERSFQ Bias Networks for Large-Scale Circuits

Dr. Gleb Krylov & Prof. Eby G. Friedman University of Rochester 12:00 PM PT · Zoom
Presents the design methodology developed for distributing bias currents across large ERSFQ circuits without static power loss. This talk accompanies the paper published in IEEE Transactions on VLSI Systems (Vol. 28, No. 11, 2020).

18Sep2023

α-Soma: SFQ Threshold Cells for Spiking Neural Networks

M. A. Karamuftuoglu & Prof. Massoud Pedram University of Southern California 12:00 PM PT · Zoom
Introduces the α-Soma cell — a single flux quantum threshold gate designed to implement spiking neural network operations at cryogenic temperatures. The design achieves sub-picojoule switching energy per synaptic event.
