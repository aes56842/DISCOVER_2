DISCoVER's superconducting chips are projected to deliver 100× the energy efficiency of CMOS at comparable performance, and 10× the processing speed at equal power draw — net of cryogenic cooling costs.1

Why It Matters

The fields that hit the hardest limits of today's hardware first.

01

AI acceleration

Large AI models consume more energy per training run than most cities use in a day. Zero-resistance hardware would let the same compute happen at a fraction of the energy — making large-scale AI sustainable long term.

02

Drug discovery

Simulating how a drug molecule binds to a protein requires thousands of parallel calculations that overwhelm CMOS systems. Superconducting logic would make personalized medicine modeling fast enough to be practical.

03

Climate modeling

Global climate simulations require compute that today's data centers can't sustain at scale. Cryogenic chips that waste almost no energy could run models at resolutions currently impossible — earlier warnings, better predictions.

Hardware this fundamental needs more people.

Superconductor electronics hasn't had its breakout moment yet — which means there's still room to define the field, not just join it. DISCoVER is a seven-university collaboration built around education and open participation, and welcomes contributors at every stage.

Undergraduate & graduate students — research areas spanning circuit design, devices, and architecture, with mentorship from faculty publishing in IEEE and DATE proceedings
Faculty & staff — collaboration across seven partner universities working across devices, circuits, architecture, and applications
Industry & community partners — outreach and engagement open to anyone interested in the future of computing hardware
